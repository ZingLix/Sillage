<h1 align="center">Sillage</h1>

<p align="center">Light up the world you've been to, little by little.</p>

![](./imgs/hero.png)

Sillage is a travel app that brings tracks, trains, flights, and hotels into one place.

You can also replay your exploration over time and watch your routes unfold as your explored area gradually grows on the map.

All your data stays local by default. It is not uploaded to a platform or used for anything else.  
If you want backup or sync, Sillage only sends your data to cloud storage or remote storage that you choose yourself.

## What You Can Do

### Maps and Tracks

- Light up the world you have actually traveled through with a fog map
- Record routes, tracks, and movement over time
- Replay how your explored area grows on a timeline

### Journeys and Records

- Record different kinds of travel data, including trains, flights, hotels, and tracks
- Import existing data automatically from travel services and apps you already use
- Use journeys to reconnect photos, flights, trains, tracks, and hotels into one complete trip

### Photos and Memories

- Put travel photos back into the trip they belong to
- Rebuild memories with time and place together
- Keep photos from becoming isolated files in your camera roll

### Achievements and Collections

- Collect rich custom travel achievements
- Go beyond administrative regions with fun themes like World Heritage sites and Civilization VI wonders
- Turn your travel history into something that feels collectible and worth revisiting

### Data and Ownership

- Keep all records on your device by default
- Avoid platform upload and unnecessary privacy concerns
- Back up or sync directly to your own OneDrive, WebDAV, or iCloud

## Agent Skill

This repository includes the `sillage-mcp` agent skill for managing Sillage records through the app's local MCP server.

```bash
npx skills add ZingLix/Sillage
```


## Why Sillage

Many travel tools are good at storing information.  
Sillage is built to preserve the shape of a journey.

Instead of breaking travel into cold, disconnected entries,  
it brings routes, places, stays, photos, and time back together  
so a trip can be remembered, revisited, and kept.
